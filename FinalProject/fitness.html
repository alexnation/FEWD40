The Basics

Fitness
Let's say you know you SHOULD go to the gym more often, but life gets in the way.
I've got great news for you - if you eat right and do the right workouts, you can work out only 1-3 times per week.
Start in the comfort of your home - check out my two favorite fitness gurus - they give you routines to do in the comfort of your home. Get a set of dumbbells from Target and get to it (and don't tell me you don't have space, if you can clear 2 square feet around you, that's all you need).

Level Up

It's time to venture outside the house for some fun classes!
If you can afford a gym membership, ClassPass is fantastic - you can try all different types of classes and gyms.
Last thing on this topic - if you're going to buy a gym membership, buy one you'll use. I used to belong to a low-priced gym chain, but I never went - it was crowded and dirty. I now go to a gym that costs a bit more but is worth every penny - I go 3-5 times/week for the incredible classes and amenities. It's an investment in my health and subsequent happiness.
At this point, I don't even exercise for weight loss. I do it to shake off the cobwebs and execute in all other areas of my life.

Nutrition

Let's get one thing out of the way: Most nutrition advice is terrible, mostly because it advocates eating more sugar than anyone needs (and sugar is hiding in absolutely everything).
That being said, I'm a fan of Michael Pollan's perspective on the matter: "Eat real food, not too much, mostly plants".
If you're looking to lose weight, check out the slow carb and ketogenic diets (both of which advocate little to no sugar).
Paleo and Whole 30 are also fantastic - they follow the "eat real food" school of thought.
